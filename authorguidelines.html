How to submit a paper to Science Ascend?

Last update: 10 June 2026

Science Ascend is a biannual, free-to-read and free-to-submit scientific journal on astrophysics research, tools, and people. This can only be realized with a steady inflow of articles from the relevant people in the field. For them to submit an appropriate article, below are the related guidelines.

Scope of Articles (Will my paper be a good fit/Is this journal a good venue?

Science Ascend's (hereinafter the Journal) covers astrophysics studies, which includes planetary, solar, stellar, galactic astrophysics, cosmology, missions/instrumentation, and both observational and theoretical studies.

The things excluded from the Journal's scope are: Earth-focusing studies and remote sensing earth observation, codes/software announcement/specifications, republication of conference proceedings/abstracts, and reviews other than the ones focusing on the last 6 months before the specific issue's publication (which means, for an issue to be published in July 2026, a review covering studies within January-June 2026 period is the only good fit).

Types of Articles

The Journal currently published in the following article types:

  • Focused recent literature review: A short literature review of the last 6 months in an astrophysics field,
  • Lecture note: A lecture note on an astrophysics-relevant topic,
  • Opinion article: A piece commenting/criticizing a peer-reviewed or preprint scientific article of the last 6 months,
  • Interview: Summarizing an interview the author made with an academician/professional from an astrophysics field,
  • Outreach: A discussion on dissemination of astrophysics scientific outputs and knowledge to the public,
  • Research report: A report on an observation campaign or a successful/failed hypothesis the author conducted recently.
